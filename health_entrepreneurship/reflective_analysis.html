Tab 3 — Reflective Analysis

Description

Across the module, our team progressed from a succinct pitch to a full proposal, stress-testing feasibility, desirability, and viability. The pitch made the case for fairness and workforce equity; the ecosystem map exposed the multi-stakeholder terrain; the proposal consolidated solution mechanics, operations, financing, and scaling.

Feelings

Initially, I was energised by the mission (equity in placements) yet apprehensive about institutional buy-in and data governance. Building the ecosystem map reduced ambiguity and turned anxiety into structured questions. Drafting the proposal transformed uncertainty into actionable plans, improving confidence in our execution pathway.

Evaluation

What worked well:

  • Clarity of problem–solution fit.
  • Phased roadmap and revenue model.
  • Evidence of systems thinking.

What was weaker:

  • Stakeholder validation depth was limited at submission stage.
  • Unit economics detail needs finer granularity.

Analysis

Entrepreneurial components for starting/growing the venture:

  • Idea formation & value proposition: MediMatch promises fairer placements that balance individual aspirations with system needs.
  • Team roles: Complementary capabilities evidenced, but future hires must add compliance and data expertise.
  • Business model & financing: B2B licensing, partnerships, subscriptions, analytics; early pilot funding staged scalability.
  • Execution strategy: MVP → pilot → partnerships → national rollout; phased marketing and ambassador programme.

Innovation environment, challenges, barriers:

  • Institutional resistance & policy cycles → mitigated by co-design pilots and publishing outcomes.
  • Data privacy & compliance (POPIA) → mitigated by privacy-by-design, audits, anonymised analytics.
  • Graduate adoption hesitation → mitigated by ambassadors, testimonials, certification incentives.

Recommendations for improvement:

  1. Strengthen stakeholder evidence with interviews and willingness-to-pay signals.
  2. Tighten unit economics with bottom-up cost models and sensitivity analysis.
  3. Pilot design with KPIs and evaluation plan.
  4. Risk rehearsal using matrix and simulations.

Conclusion

The artefacts chart a clear learning arc from persuasive storytelling (pitch) to systemic diagnosis (ecosystem) to integrated venture design (proposal). I can now articulate and interrogate a venture across desirability, feasibility, and viability, and show how systems thinking informs pragmatic sequencing and risk management.

Action Plan

Implementation Timeline:

  • Immediate (2–4 weeks): Stakeholder interviews; refine unit economics; privacy impact assessment.
  • Short term (4–8 weeks): Pilot MoU; ambassador recruitment; develop metrics dashboard mock-ups.
  • Medium term (8–16 weeks): Pilot execution; iterate algorithm weights; prepare adoption case study.
