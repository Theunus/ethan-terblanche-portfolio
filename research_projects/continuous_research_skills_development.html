Continuous Research Skills Development

FAMH4013A - Research Project

Throughout this course, I have developed a clearer understanding of my strengths and areas for growth across key research competencies. Reflecting on my progress, I identified the following areas:

  • Information literacy: I have improved significantly in locating credible sources, using academic databases, and evaluating the quality of evidence.
  • Referencing: My confidence in applying consistent referencing techniques has grown, although I continue to refine accuracy and attention to detail.
  • Analytical reasoning: I have become more capable of interpreting literature critically, recognising patterns, and synthesising ideas.
  • Academic writing: My writing has become more structured, focused, and aligned with academic conventions.
  • Time management: Working full time while completing an Honours degree strengthened my ability to plan, prioritise, and manage competing demands.

From these competencies, I selected Information Literacy and Analytical Reasoning as key areas for further development. Both were essential for my research topic on predictive AI models for early burnout detection and will continue to support my future academic and professional work.

Competency 1: Information Literacy

Why I Selected This Resource

I chose the JBI workshop because it offered structured training in evidence synthesis, critical appraisal, and systematic approaches to locating and evaluating research. These are essential components of information literacy. The workshop is recognised internationally for its research standards, and attending it provided both credibility and practical skills. It also aligned closely with my research focus, which required me to work with complex literature on burnout, AI technologies, and health systems.

Reflection on Skill Development

The JBI workshop significantly enhanced my ability to navigate academic databases, refine search strategies, and assess the quality and relevance of sources. It helped me distinguish between strong evidence and weaker forms of information, and gave me tools to screen literature more efficiently. I became more confident in identifying gaps, extracting key insights, and evaluating the methodological strength of studies. Although I have strengthened this competency, I recognise that information literacy requires continuous practice. I still aim to improve my ability to manage large bodies of literature more efficiently, use advanced database filters confidently, and incorporate evidence synthesis techniques more consistently in future projects. I also hope to continue using JBI tools to deepen my understanding of critical appraisal in AI-related health research.

Competency 2: Analytical Reasoning

Why I Selected This Resource

I selected the course readings and methodology materials as key resources because they improved my understanding of how to think critically about research design, logic, and evidence. These materials helped me analyse research beyond surface-level descriptions and understand why certain methodological choices are appropriate. This was particularly important for my topic, which required me to consider AI bias, ethical implications, and burnout as a systemic issue.

Reflection on Skill Development

Throughout the course, my analytical reasoning improved as I learned how to interpret literature more deeply, question assumptions, and connect concepts across different fields. I became more confident in identifying relationships between variables, evaluating the strength of arguments, and recognising limitations in existing studies. This competency helped me strengthen my research proposal, improve the coherence of my final work, and engage more thoughtfully with the ethical challenges of predictive AI. Even though I have developed stronger analytical reasoning skills, I aim to continue strengthening this competency by engaging with more advanced readings, practicing deeper critical comparisons between studies, and improving my ability to analyse data-driven research. I also hope to develop greater confidence in applying analytical thinking to real-world organisational and health system problems.
