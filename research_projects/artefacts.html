Research Proposal

Why I Selected This Artefact

I selected my research proposal as an artefact because it represents a key developmental stage in my journey as an emerging researcher. The proposal brought together the foundational components of the research process — problem formulation, literature engagement, methodology, ethics, and feasibility.

Reflection on the Artefact

Reflecting on my research proposal allowed me to see how much I grew in my ability to conceptualise and plan a research study. Writing the proposal required me to move beyond general interest and engage deeply with existing literature on burnout, predictive analytics, clinical workload pressures, and digital health innovations. This pushed me to understand the broader systemic context within which burnout occurs, as well as the opportunities and limitations of using artificial intelligence as an early detection tool. The proposal challenged me to think critically about how to frame a research problem. I had to narrow the focus, define key concepts clearly, and explain why early burnout detection matters not only for individual medical doctors but also for the wider health system. This process strengthened my understanding of research significance, and I learned to articulate the value of the study in a way that aligned with current global health priorities. Developing the methodology section was another significant learning experience. I had to consider different research designs, the suitability of each method, and the practical realities of studying predictive AI models in a healthcare context. This required careful thinking about feasibility, data requirements, analytical approaches, and ethical considerations such as privacy, risk minimisation, and responsible use of digital health technologies. The proposal helped me understand why methodological alignment is essential for producing meaningful and trustworthy findings. In writing this artefact, I also realised how central ethics is to research involving AI and healthcare professionals. The proposal required me to think through issues such as data sensitivity, confidentiality, algorithmic fairness, and informed consent. This expanded my understanding of the researcher’s responsibility beyond academic requirements and into the ethical complexities of technology-driven health research. The proposal also highlighted the iterative nature of research. It showed me that ideas evolve, and that developing a research plan involves reflection, revision, and refinement. Feedback from supervisors further shaped the proposal, helping me strengthen my academic reasoning, improve clarity, and ensure stronger alignment between the research question, literature review, and methodological approach. This artefact represents a foundational step in becoming a more confident and capable researcher. It marks the point where I transitioned from conceptual interest to structured academic planning, and it reflects the depth of engagement required to design a coherent, ethical, and relevant research study on predictive AI models for early burnout detection among medical doctors.

Ethics Application

Why I Selected This Artefact

I selected this artefact because engaging with ethics was a critical part of my development as a researcher. Ethical considerations shape the entire research process, especially in studies involving sensitive data, healthcare professionals, and emerging technologies such as predictive AI.

Why I Selected This Artefact

I selected my research proposal as an artefact because it represents a key developmental stage in my journey as an emerging researcher. The proposal brought together the foundational components of the research process — problem formulation, literature engagement, methodology, ethics, and feasibility.

Reflection on the Artefact

Reflecting on my ethics application and the discussions that informed it has been one of the most important parts of my research journey. My topic, predictive AI models for early burnout detection among medical doctors, required me to think beyond traditional ethical considerations and engage with more complex issues related to technology, data governance, and professional wellbeing. Through the ethics process, I learned that research involving AI and healthcare workers carries unique sensitivities. Burnout is a deeply personal experience, and predictive modelling requires data that may include indicators of stress, workload, performance, or behavioural patterns. This raised important questions for me about privacy, confidentiality, and the potential unintended consequences of early identification, such as stigma, pressure, or misuse of data. Ethical discussions helped me understand that protecting participants goes beyond consent forms and requires an awareness of how algorithms may shape decisions, perceptions, and outcomes. I also became more aware of issues related to algorithmic fairness. Literature on AI ethics highlights the risk of bias in data-driven models, and this influenced how I approached the ethics component. It made me consider how datasets are constructed, who controls the technology, and how decisions based on AI outputs may impact individuals differently. This expanded my understanding of ethics as a continuous process rather than a single administrative step. Completing the ethics application also strengthened my ability to plan a study responsibly. I had to clearly articulate the purpose of the research, justify the need for data, and consider mechanisms for protecting confidentiality. I learned how to identify potential risks and propose mitigation strategies, such as secure data handling, anonymisation techniques, and transparent communication with participants. This reinforced the idea that ethical research requires intentional planning from the earliest stages of a project. The discussions with supervisors and peers further shaped my awareness of ethical complexities. Our conversations helped me think about power dynamics, the role of institutions, and the importance of ensuring that research outcomes do not create harm, even unintentionally. These discussions also aligned with principles introduced in the GOLD01 supervision course, which emphasised responsible scholarship, reflexivity, and respect in the research relationship. This artefact represents how I have grown in my understanding of ethical practice in research. It reflects a shift from seeing ethics as compliance to recognising it as a central pillar of responsible, reflective, and human-centred scholarship. Engaging deeply with ethics has strengthened my ability to approach future research with sensitivity, awareness, and a commitment to safeguarding the wellbeing and dignity of those involved.

Final Research

Why I Selected This Artefact

I selected my final research as an artefact because it represents the culmination of my entire learning journey in this course.

Reflection on the Artefact

Reflecting on my final research allowed me to appreciate the progress I made throughout the research cycle. Completing this project, even at a conceptual level, required sustained effort, analytical thinking, and the integration of theoretical and practical insights. As I revisited the proposal, literature, and ethical considerations, I realised how each step contributed to shaping the final study into a coherent and meaningful piece of academic work. One of the key insights I gained while working on the final research was the complexity of studying burnout and the importance of framing it as both an individual experience and a systemic challenge. Literature consistently highlights burnout among medical doctors as a global concern linked to workload, organisational culture, emotional strain, and demanding clinical environments. Engaging with these sources deepened my understanding of how multi-layered the issue is, and why early detection is valuable not only for individual wellbeing but also for supporting stable, safe, and sustainable health systems. Integrating artificial intelligence into the study introduced a different layer of complexity. The final research required me to think carefully about the opportunities and limitations of predictive modelling. I had to consider issues such as data quality, model accuracy, contextual relevance, and the importance of avoiding bias. This encouraged me to approach the topic with balanced reasoning, recognising both the promise of AI-driven early detection and the ethical, practical, and methodological challenges involved. Working through these considerations strengthened my ability to think critically and evaluate the feasibility of innovative health solutions. Completing the final research also highlighted the importance of academic writing and the process of refining ideas through multiple drafts. I learned how to synthesise literature more effectively, write with greater clarity, and structure an argument in a way that aligns with the expectations of scholarly work. This reflects a significant shift from the early stages of the course, where I often focused on content alone, by the end, I had developed a stronger sense of academic voice, structure, and coherence. The supervision process played an important role in shaping the final research, and completing the project reminded me of how valuable feedback and guidance are in strengthening academic work. I learned to view supervision as collaboration and refinement rather than correction. The GOLD01 course reinforced this, helping me engage with feedback intentionally and with a clearer understanding of my responsibilities as a developing researcher. This artefact represents much more than the final product, it symbolises the full arc of my development. It reflects growth in confidence, academic maturity, ethical awareness, methodological understanding, and my emerging research identity. Completing the final research has given me a greater appreciation for the discipline of research and a stronger sense of readiness to engage in future academic or professional projects that require critical inquiry and evidence-informed reasoning.
