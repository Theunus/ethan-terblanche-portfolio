Internship Report

Context

This artefact is my full internship report submitted as part of FAMH4017A. It documents my responsibilities, achievements, challenges, skills development, and reflections during my time as a Project Coordinator at NWU. It includes detailed descriptions of my contributions to major projects such as the brand video and recruitment campaign, as well as the feedback I received from colleagues and clients. This artefact is included because it gives a complete overview of my workplace experience and provides the strongest evidence of my competency development across the year .

Reflection

Writing this report helped me understand the depth of my growth during the internship. I realised how much responsibility I had managed and how much I had learned about project coordination, stakeholder engagement, and brand communication. Reflecting on the descriptions of deadlines, challenges, and project outcomes helped me recognise the improvement in my confidence and ability to manage complexity. I also noticed that many of the skills listed in the report align with core health systems competencies, such as collaboration, communication, systems thinking, and accountability. Engaging with this artefact reminded me that my work contributed to the university's broader strategic goals and strengthened my readiness for professional practice.

Organisational Culture Assignment

Context

This artefact is an analytical assignment exploring the organisational culture within the Marketing and Student Recruitment environment at NWU. The task required me to examine concepts such as shared values, behaviours, structure, and communication patterns within my department. I selected this artefact because it demonstrates how I engaged analytically with my workplace environment and linked real experiences to academic theory on organisational culture .

Reflection

This assignment helped me observe my department with more intention. I recognised how NWU's culture blends structure with creativity. I saw how formal procedures guided workflow yet still allowed collaboration and innovation during campaign development. Applying academic frameworks made these everyday interactions more meaningful. It also highlighted the impact of leadership styles on team dynamics. Reflecting on culture increased my appreciation for psychological safety, transparency, and communication in fostering productivity. This artefact strengthened my understanding of how organisational culture influences service delivery and employee experience within large institutions.

Diversity in the Workplace Assignment

Context

This artefact examines how diversity manifests within my department at NWU and evaluates strategies for fostering inclusion. It includes my observations on cultural, linguistic, and professional diversity within the Marketing and Student Recruitment unit. I included this artefact because it reflects my understanding of diversity, equity, and inclusion in real work environments and how these factors affect collaboration, innovation, and staff morale .

Reflection

Engaging with this assignment helped me appreciate the richness of diversity in my team. I realised that diversity extends beyond visible characteristics and includes differences in thinking, communication, and experience. Reflecting on my own department's strengths and gaps deepened my awareness of how inclusive environments are intentionally built rather than assumed. The assignment encouraged me to think about active mentorship, communication practices, and cultural celebration as pathways to strengthen inclusion. This reflection shaped my understanding of leadership responsibility in promoting fairness and belonging.

Observational Assignment: Conflict Resolution

Context

This artefact analyses a conflict scenario between two interdependent teams within the Marketing and Student Recruitment division. It explores the root cause of the conflict, evaluates the effectiveness of the response, and applies academic conflict management frameworks such as the Thomas Kilmann model. I included this artefact because conflict is an inevitable part of organisational life, and learning how to address it constructively is essential for professional development .

Reflection

This assignment taught me that conflict is not necessarily negative but becomes harmful when communication is unclear or expectations are misaligned. Reflecting on the incident reinforced the importance of active listening, emotional intelligence, and transparent communication. Applying academic frameworks helped me understand that collaborative approaches often produce longer lasting solutions. This artefact strengthened my appreciation for early intervention and structured problem solving, both of which are critical in health and organisational systems.

Observational Assignment: Leadership Styles Assignment

Context

This artefact examines the leadership styles I observed within my department and compares them to leadership approaches in the South African health system. It also includes a personal reflection on the leadership style I aspire to develop. I selected this artefact because leadership is a core health system competency and because my internship environment provided rich examples of effective leadership practice .

Reflection

Reflecting on leadership helped me recognise how much I learned from my line manager's democratic and transformational style. I realised how impactful leadership becomes when it builds confidence, encourages participation, and supports autonomy. Comparing university leadership to healthcare leadership strengthened my understanding that leadership approaches must suit their context. This artefact encouraged me to consider how empathy, communication, and vision shape team culture and outcomes. It also informed my aspiration to develop a servant transformational leadership style rooted in trust, inclusion, and accountability.
