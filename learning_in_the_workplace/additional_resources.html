Additional Resources

Context

This section contains curated resources that supported my learning throughout my internship. These resources helped me understand workplace dynamics, improve my communication and leadership skills, and deepen my understanding of project coordination.

Emotional Intelligence in the Workplace – Daniel Goleman

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y7m9eNoB3NU

Why included:

This video introduces the core elements of emotional intelligence, which include self awareness, empathy, and relationship management. These skills were essential in my role as a Project Coordinator, especially when navigating client expectations and handling conflict. Understanding emotional intelligence supported my ability to communicate calmly, listen actively, and respond professionally in stressful situations.

Leadership Styles Explained – Practical Psychology

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9OL1df0u9Lk

Why included:

This resource provides a clear breakdown of different leadership styles such as democratic, transformational, and autocratic approaches. It helped me better understand the leadership style within my department at NWU and supported my reflection on the type of leader I aspire to become. The video strengthened my understanding of how leadership can influence team culture, motivation, and workflow.

Systems Thinking for Beginners – Crash Course

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K9kiHOzAu9s

Why included:

Systems thinking helped me understand how different units within the university interact and influence each other. This video explains how complex systems operate as interconnected parts. It supported my ability to see how marketing, branding, student recruitment, and organisational decision making fit into the broader university system, which is essential for Health Systems Science.

Harvard Business Review – How to Manage Up

https://hbr.org/video/5829984785001/how-to-manage-up

Why included:

Managing up refers to communicating effectively with supervisors and senior staff. This resource helped me improve my professionalism when presenting updates, negotiating timelines, or seeking advice from senior managers. It supported my confidence when interacting with leadership and helped me build stronger professional relationships.

Psychological Safety – Amy Edmondson (TED Talk)

https://www.ted.com/talks/amy_edmondson_how_to_turn_a_group_of_strangers_into_a_team

Why included:

Psychological safety was a key part of the supportive team environment in my department. This talk explains why people perform better when they feel safe to ask questions, admit mistakes, or share ideas. It helped me understand why our team worked well together and reinforced the leadership qualities I admired in my line manager.

Fun Walk for Student Fees

Context

This artefact represents a workplace initiative that I helped organise with colleagues in the Marketing and Student Recruitment division. The Fun Walk was a collaborative effort aimed at raising funds for students with outstanding fees who were at risk of exclusion or were unable to register for the new academic year. The event brought together staff members across departments who wanted to contribute towards student welfare in a meaningful way. I included this artefact because it reflects not only my project coordination involvement but also the values-driven culture in our division where supporting students extends beyond standard operational duties.

My role in the Fun Walk involved planning logistics with colleagues, assisting with communication about the event, and ensuring that participants understood the purpose of the initiative. I contributed to organising the route, helping with registrations, and coordinating refreshments and materials. This experience demonstrated how teamwork and social responsibility can intersect with professional responsibilities in a university environment.

Reflection

This event became an important moment in my internship journey because it allowed me to engage with colleagues around a cause that extended beyond daily tasks. The Fun Walk reminded me of the human impact behind our work in student recruitment. While my role often focuses on marketing, branding, and communication, this initiative allowed me to support students in a direct and compassionate way.

Working on the Fun Walk helped me understand the importance of social accountability within higher education institutions. Universities play a vital role in supporting students who face financial difficulties, and participating in this initiative made me more aware of the structural challenges students encounter when fees become a barrier to academic progression. Being part of a fundraising event taught me that even small collective actions can make a meaningful difference when a community is committed to helping others.

The planning process strengthened my teamwork, communication, and event coordination skills. I learned how to collaborate with colleagues across different levels of seniority and how to adapt quickly when plans needed adjustment. Seeing my colleagues come together to walk for a shared purpose created a strong sense of unity and reinforced the values of empathy, care, and service that I observed throughout my time at NWU.

This artefact is important to me because it reflects the heart of my internship experience. It showed me that professional roles within a university are not limited to administrative or operational tasks. They also involve contributing to a supportive environment where staff act as advocates for student success. The Fun Walk encouraged me to think more deeply about the type of professional I want to become. I want to be someone who not only meets organisational objectives but also remains guided by compassion and a commitment to student wellbeing.
