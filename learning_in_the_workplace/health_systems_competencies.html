Health Systems Competencies

Context

This section reflects the competencies I developed during my internship and how these align with Health Systems Science.

1. Communication

Working as a central coordinator between clients and designers sharpened my communication skills. I learned to clarify expectations, summarise instructions, and respond professionally under pressure. My internship report shows extensive written and verbal communication across stakeholders .

2. Collaboration

My role required daily collaboration across departments and campuses. Through campaign work, photoshoots, and feedback cycles, I gained experience in teamwork, shared problem solving, and cross functional coordination.

3. Systems Thinking

I developed an understanding of how brand, design, communication, and recruitment link into the university's strategic goals. Reading NWU's mission and strategy documents as part of my internship report helped me see the bigger system and how my work contributed to it .

4. Leadership

Through observing my line manager and managing smaller tasks independently, I developed early leadership competencies such as decision making, initiative, and supportive communication.

5. Ethical Practice

I handled confidential information, brand assets, and official communication with care. I ensured accuracy, fairness, and brand compliance in every project I delivered.
