About My Internship

Context

My internship took place within the Marketing and Student Recruitment division at the North West University. Although the accredited internship module formally began in June 2025, I worked in the organisation from January to October 2025. I was based in the Brand and Design unit as a Project Coordinator. My responsibilities spanned project management, client communication, content coordination, workflow support, and brand quality assurance. These responsibilities are described in detail in my internship report .

My role required daily engagement with designers, copywriters, videographers, faculty stakeholders, senior managers, and student assistants. Over time, I became the central contact point for the intake and coordination of design requests across the university. I also gained experience supporting strategic projects, including the NWU brand video, the 2026 recruitment campaign, institutional photoshoots, and stakeholder liaison across three campuses.

Reflection

Working full time in this role shaped my professional identity in ways that would not have been possible through coursework alone. The environment taught me how to plan projects, manage expectations, and handle pressure while maintaining professionalism. I learned how marketing supports the university's strategic goals, particularly its focus on student recruitment and brand consistency. Through daily interactions with colleagues and clients, I discovered that I value work that combines creativity, organisation, and relationship building. The experience also strengthened qualities such as resilience, patience, and adaptability. These insights shaped the way I see myself as a future health systems professional who can bridge operational, interpersonal, and strategic demands.
