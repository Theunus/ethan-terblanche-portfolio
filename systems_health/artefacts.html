Health Financing Reforms (Activity 2.4.2 – Egypt)

(Add document named Act 2.4.2)

Linked Health Systems Science Competency: Health Policy, Financing, and Systems Strengthening

This artefact explores Egypt’s health financing reforms and provided me with a deeper understanding of how countries manage financial resources to move towards universal health coverage. Through this activity, I learned about the importance of raising revenue sustainably, pooling funds efficiently, and purchasing services strategically to achieve equity and access. Analysing Egypt’s approach helped me see how financial mechanisms, such as universal health insurance and strategic purchasing, can reduce health disparities and improve accountability.

Reflecting on this learning, I realised that effective health financing reform is not just about funding healthcare but about building a system that distributes resources fairly and transparently. I was particularly interested in how Egypt’s Universal Health Insurance law introduced gradual coverage expansion and targeted subsidies for vulnerable groups. This highlighted the need for financial protection policies that consider both direct and indirect costs faced by patients.

Connecting this to the South African context helped me understand how health financing principles can be adapted to local needs. The exercise encouraged me to think about how lessons from Egypt could inform the development of South Africa’s National Health Insurance system, especially in defining benefit packages, ensuring strong governance, and maintaining accountability. I also reflected on how these financial strategies align with systems health thinking, where sustainable reform depends on collaboration, equity, and ongoing evaluation.

This activity reinforced my appreciation for the complexity of financing health systems and showed me how financial policy shapes the broader goals of health equity and universal coverage. It has encouraged me to continue developing my understanding of health economics and policy so that I can contribute more effectively to future health system improvement initiatives.

Module Reflections

Budget Matters for Health

My personal takeaway from the session on budget matters for health is a much deeper appreciation of how financial planning influences every level of the health system. Before this session, I saw budgeting mainly as a technical or administrative process. Through the discussion, I began to understand that budgets represent priorities and values, showing how resources are distributed to meet health needs fairly and effectively. I realised that financial management is not only about numbers but also about making decisions that impact people’s access to care and the overall functioning of the system.

This session helped me to think more critically about how limited resources can be managed responsibly to achieve the greatest benefit for communities. I learned that effective budgeting requires both transparency and adaptability, as circumstances in healthcare often change. I also recognised that financial planning should include input from different stakeholders to ensure that funds are used where they are needed most. This understanding encouraged me to view budgeting as a shared process that depends on collaboration, foresight, and accountability.

From the perspective of my work placement, I can see how these principles apply in practice. During my placement, I observed how departments plan their spending for training, equipment, and community outreach. I noticed that when budgeting decisions are made with clear goals and communication, they have a positive effect on service delivery and staff morale. This experience reinforced the idea that financial planning supports not only efficiency but also equity within the system.

Understanding Cash Flow Statements

Learning about cash flow statements has given me a much clearer understanding of how financial management supports the stability and sustainability of healthcare organisations. I realised that cash flow is not only about tracking income and expenses but about ensuring that resources are available at the right time to maintain operations and deliver quality care. Understanding how funds move in and out of an organisation has helped me see how financial planning influences decisions around staffing, equipment, and service delivery. It also highlighted how important it is for healthcare managers to anticipate challenges and manage resources carefully to avoid interruptions in care.

This topic made me more aware of the link between financial transparency and accountability. A well-maintained cash flow statement provides insight into the organisation’s overall health and helps leaders make informed decisions. I now see that financial information is not just for accountants or managers, but for all professionals who contribute to the effective functioning of the system. It strengthened my understanding of how financial literacy supports collaboration and helps teams work towards common goals.

Working in a Team of Six

Working in a team of six during this module was a valuable learning experience that helped me understand both the challenges and rewards of collaboration in a professional setting. Each member brought different strengths, perspectives, and ways of thinking, which made our discussions and outputs more balanced and creative. I realised that teamwork in health systems, much like in our group, depends on effective communication, respect for diversity, and a shared sense of purpose.

Through this experience, I learned the importance of listening actively and recognising the value of each person’s contribution. At times, coordinating tasks and aligning ideas required patience and flexibility, but these moments helped me develop stronger interpersonal and problem-solving skills. I also gained a better understanding of how leadership and collaboration coexist, effective teamwork is not about one person taking control, but about creating space for everyone to contribute and feel responsible for the outcome.

I found that when we divided tasks according to our strengths, the work became more efficient and the final product reflected a combination of everyone’s effort. I also noticed how constructive feedback and mutual support built confidence within the group. This reminded me that professional collaboration in healthcare relies on trust, open communication, and shared accountability.

Reflecting on this experience, I see how teamwork aligns with the values of health systems science, particularly systems thinking and interprofessional collaboration. Just as different components of a system must work together to function effectively, a team succeeds when individuals work collectively towards a common goal. This experience has strengthened my confidence in group work and shown me how collaboration contributes to learning, innovation, and professional growth.
